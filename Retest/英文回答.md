## 毕业设计

CN：我的毕业论文题为《基于机器学习的华西雨屏区土壤容重空间分布特征研究》，属于环境科学与工程专业。华西雨屏区是我国重要生态屏障，但面临水土流失和土地退化问题。土壤容重是评估土壤质量的关键，影响植被生长和水分渗透。传统测量方法费时且覆盖范围有限。为此，我结合机器学习、遥感数据和环境变量，研究该区土壤容重分布及其驱动因素，为生态恢复提供科学依据。

我的研究通过综合地面观测、机器学习和遥感观测三种方法，探讨了华西雨屏区土壤容重的空间分布特征。地面观测包括现场采样和理化分析，机器学习方法采用了随机森林、XGBoost 和线性回归模型等，遥感观测则利用了 Sentinel 和 Landsat8 卫星数据。研究考虑了多种环境因子，如地形因子（高程、坡度、曲率）、气候条件（降雨、温度、蒸发）、植被覆盖（NDVI、LAI、FVC）和土壤属性（类型、质地、有机质）。研究内容主要包括空间特征模型构建、空间分布特征分析以及空间变化驱动机制解析，旨在揭示华西雨屏区土壤容重的空间分布特征。目前已经完成了地面采样、遥感数据获取与处理、随机森林模拟和 XGboost 模拟，还有开题报告和综述的撰写。

EN：My graduation thesis is titled "Research on Spatial Distribution Characteristics of Soil Bulk Density in the Huaxi Rain Screen Region Based on Machine Learning" (Environmental Science and Engineering). The Huaxi Rain Screen Region, a crucial ecological barrier in China, is currently challenged by soil erosion and land degradation. Soil bulk density, a key indicator for assessing soil quality, significantly affects vegetation growth and water infiltration. Traditional measurement methods are time-consuming and spatially limited. To address these limitations, this study integrates machine learning, remote sensing data, and environmental variables to investigate the spatial distribution of soil bulk density and its driving factors, aiming to provide scientific evidence for ecological restoration.

This research employs a comprehensive approach combining ground-based observations, machine learning, and remote sensing monitoring to explore the spatial distribution characteristics of soil bulk density in the Huaxi Rain Screen Region. Ground observations include field sampling and physicochemical analysis. Machine learning methodologies utilize Random Forest, XGBoost, and linear regression models. Remote sensing observations are based on Sentinel and Landsat8 satellite data. The study incorporates diverse environmental factors such as topographic parameters (elevation, slope, curvature), climatic conditions (precipitation, temperature, evaporation), vegetation indices (NDVI, LAI, FVC), and soil properties (type, texture, organic matter). The research focuses on constructing spatial prediction models, analyzing spatial distribution patterns, and elucidating the driving mechanisms underlying spatial variations, ultimately aiming to reveal the spatial distribution characteristics of soil bulk density. Current progress includes completion of field sampling, acquisition and processing of remote sensing data, implementation of Random Forest and XGBoost simulations, and drafting of the research proposal and literature review.
